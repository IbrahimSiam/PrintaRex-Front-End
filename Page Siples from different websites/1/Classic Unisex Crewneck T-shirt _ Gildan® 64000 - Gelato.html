Tips and guides

DTG (Direct-to-garment )
DTF (Direct-to-film)

Overview

Direct-to-Garment (DTG) printing applies water-based ink directly onto fabrics using advanced inkjet technology. This process delivers vibrant, highly detailed designs and is ideal for intricate artwork or photo-realistic prints.

Design Specs

  • File formats: PNG or JPEG.
  • Minimum resolution: 150 DPI.
  • Color profile: sRGB.
  • Backgrounds: transparent.
  • If using our templates, make sure you remove all guide layers.

Design Best Practices

  • Transparency: Avoid semi-transparent elements; use solid colors or halftoning techniques instead.
  • Resolution: Low-resolution images must be recreated, not resized.
  • Color Handling: For black garments, leave black areas transparent to prevent muted underbase effects.
  • Borders: Avoid exact borders as minor shifts during production can distort them.

Key Notes

  • White garments remain unprinted in white areas.
  • Bright garment colors may slightly tint light tones.
  • Manual handling might result in small placement variations.

Care Instructions

  • Wash garments inside out with cold water to preserve the design.
  • Use mild detergent and avoid bleach.
  • Air dry or tumble dry on low heat.
  • Avoid ironing directly on the design.
